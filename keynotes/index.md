## Dr. Krithika Iyer

Dr. Krithika Iyer is a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Sheikh Zayed Institute for Pediatric Surgical Innovation at Children’s National Hospital in Washington, D.C., where she works in the Precision Medical Imaging Laboratory under the mentorship of Dr. Marius George Linguraru. Her research focuses on computational anatomy, medical image analysis, statistical shape modeling, and machine learning methods for understanding anatomical variability from medical imaging data.

She earned her Ph.D. from the Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute at the University of Utah, where she was advised by Dr. Shireen Elhabian. Prior to her doctoral studies, she received her Bachelor of Engineering from the Maharashtra Institute of Technology, University of Pune, and gained industry experience as an Associate System Engineer at IBM Global Business Services.

Dr. Iyer’s work has contributed significantly to advancing shape analysis from complex anatomical data. She has led and co developed methods such as Mesh2SSM and Mesh2SSM++, frameworks for learning statistical shape models from anatomical surface meshes, as well as ScorP, a statistics informed dense correspondence prediction framework for unsegmented medical images. Her research combines statistical learning, geometric modeling, and deep learning to create interpretable and anatomically meaningful representations of biological structures.

Her research has appeared in leading venues including MICCAI, MIUA, Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology, and ShapeMI workshops. She received the Best Paper Award in Medical Images and Computational Models at MIUA 2024 and has actively contributed to the medical imaging community through conference reviewing and academic leadership roles.
