## Dr. Krithika Iyer

<img src="/images/Krithika_Iyer.jpg" alt="Dr. Krithika Iyer" style="width: 100%; border-radius: 12px;">

<div style="flex: 0 0 240px;">

<div style="flex: 1; min-width: 280px;">

Dr. Krithika Iyer is a researcher in computational anatomy, medical image analysis, and statistical shape modeling, currently affiliated with the Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute and the Kahlert School of Computing at the University of Utah. Her work focuses on developing probabilistic and deep learning based methods for understanding anatomical variability from medical imaging data, with applications spanning computational medicine, morphology analysis, and population level anatomical modeling.

Her research has contributed significantly to advancing shape analysis directly from complex anatomical data. She has led and co developed methods such as Mesh2SSM and Mesh2SSM++, frameworks for learning statistical shape models from anatomical surface meshes, as well as ScorP, a statistics informed dense correspondence prediction framework for unsegmented medical images. Her work combines statistical learning, geometric modeling, and machine learning to create interpretable and anatomically meaningful representations of biological structures.

Dr. Iyer’s research has appeared in leading venues including MICCAI, MIUA, Frontiers in Bioengineering and Biotechnology, and ShapeMI workshops. She received the Best Paper Award in Medical Images and Computational Models at MIUA 2024 and has actively contributed to the medical imaging community through conference reviewing and academic leadership roles.
